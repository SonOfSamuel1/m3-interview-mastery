## SECTION ONE: CREATING YOUR MASS MOVEMENT

---

### Secret #1: The Charismatic Leader / Attractive Character

#### Overcome Imposter Syndrome
- [ ] Recognize that leadership is developed through mastery and contribution
- [ ] Understand you only need to be 1-2 steps ahead of your audience
- [ ] First master something for yourself, then transition to contribution

#### Embrace Your Calling to Serve
- [ ] Ignore the voice telling you you're inadequate
- [ ] Recognize people are waiting for your guidance
- [ ] Step into your calling despite fear and self-doubt

#### Identify Your Attractive Character Identity
- [ ] Choose one of four identities:
  - [ ] The Leader (driven by conviction, has answers)
  - [ ] The Adventurer/Crusader (driven by curiosity and discovery)
  - [ ] The Evangelist/Reporter (discovers secrets by interviewing experts)
  - [ ] The Reluctant Hero (shy and humble, shares out of duty)

#### Live the Life Your Audience Wishes They Could Live
- [ ] Build the life you teach others to build
- [ ] Achieve the results you help others achieve
- [ ] Live the values you promote
- [ ] Share your lifestyle authentically

#### Build a Relatable Backstory
- [ ] Document who you were before your transformation
- [ ] Identify what struggles you faced
- [ ] Describe what challenged you
- [ ] Explain how you are different now

#### Develop Core Elements as an Attractive Character
- [ ] Create your Backstory (makes audiences care and feel connected)
- [ ] Develop Parables and Stories (communicate philosophy through narrative)
- [ ] Share Character Flaws (vulnerabilities and mistakes)
- [ ] Establish Polarity (take clear stands on controversial topics)
- [ ] Create Unique Vocabulary (terminology specific to your framework)

#### Choose Who You Want to Serve
- [ ] Select from three core markets:
  - [ ] Wealth (money and business)
  - [ ] Health (fitness, wellness, disease prevention)
  - [ ] Relationships (dating, marriage, family, social)

#### Find Your Niche Position Within Your Market
- [ ] Research your market and identify other experts
- [ ] Find a way to complement their work rather than compete
- [ ] Identify the gap where you can provide unique value

#### Maintain Absolute Certainty
- [ ] Speak with conviction even before you have all the answers
- [ ] Present your ideas as if they're proven truths
- [ ] Don't apologize for your beliefs or methodology
- [ ] Back up your certainty with evidence and results

#### Build Through Contribution and Service
- [ ] Work for free to develop skills and test ideas
- [ ] Get real results for your initial audience
- [ ] Capture stories and testimonials documenting results
- [ ] Build your platform on genuine success

#### Develop Authentic Emotional Intelligence
- [ ] Listen actively to your audience's fears, dreams, and desires
- [ ] Show genuine empathy for their struggles
- [ ] Connect on a personal, human level
- [ ] Use storytelling to create emotional bridges

#### Build Toward 1,000 True Fans
- [ ] Focus on creating tremendous value for a smaller, dedicated audience
- [ ] Build deep relationships with your followers
- [ ] Continue until growth naturally accelerates

#### Don't Let Imperfection Stop You
- [ ] Start now, not when you're perfect
- [ ] Do it over and over until it becomes comfortable
- [ ] Become who you need to be through practice and repetition

### Secret #2: The Cause

#### Understand the Three Components of a Mass Movement
- [ ] Identify your role as an expert or guide
- [ ] Define your new opportunity (the vehicle for change)
- [ ] Create a future-based cause that unites the tribe

#### Create a Vision of a Better Future
- [ ] Describe the future state after your audience has succeeded
- [ ] Show them what their life will look like when they achieve their goals
- [ ] Make this vision inspiring, achievable, and emotionally compelling

#### Identify the "Enemy" or False Belief System to Fight
- [ ] Identify the primary false belief that keeps people stuck
- [ ] Position your movement as the antidote
- [ ] Unite your community around defeating this false belief

#### Create a Memorable Slogan
- [ ] Create a short, punchy phrase that captures your cause
- [ ] Make it something your community can adopt to identify themselves
- [ ] Ensure it's memorable and repeatable
- [ ] Examples: "Lady Boss," "Funnel Hacker," "Life Changer"

#### Develop Core Values for Your Movement
- [ ] Create 5-7 core values such as:
  - [ ] Never gives up
  - [ ] Gives no excuses
  - [ ] Pushes through
  - [ ] Gets the job done
  - [ ] Takes action
  - [ ] Is confident
  - [ ] Loves themselves

#### Create Visual and Verbal Identity
- [ ] Design a logo or visual symbol
- [ ] Create consistent color schemes
- [ ] Develop specific language and terminology
- [ ] Use these elements consistently across all materials

#### Position Your Cause as Aspirational
- [ ] Frame membership as an achievement or status symbol
- [ ] Show how adopting your cause transforms identity
- [ ] Make people want to be part of your community

#### Tell Stories That Reinforce Your Cause
- [ ] Share customer transformation stories that showcase core values
- [ ] Highlight community members who exemplify the movement
- [ ] Tell before-and-after stories of embracing your cause

#### Create an "Us vs. Them" Dynamic (Respectfully)
- [ ] Articulate what makes your movement different from status quo
- [ ] Show how your cause challenges conventional wisdom
- [ ] Attack the false belief system, not competitors directly

#### Give Your Community a Reason to Stay
- [ ] Create a community where members can connect
- [ ] Host regular events, webinars, or gatherings
- [ ] Recognize and celebrate members who embody cause values

#### Make Your Cause Bigger Than Profit
- [ ] Focus on impact and transformation
- [ ] Frame your work as a mission or calling
- [ ] Appeal to desire to be part of something meaningful

#### Use Your Cause to Pre-Filter Your Audience
- [ ] Be clear and specific about your cause
- [ ] Don't try to appeal to everyone
- [ ] Celebrate people who embrace your cause fully

### Secret #3: The New Opportunity

#### Understand the Three Phases of Market Evolution
- [ ] Phase 1: Repair (fixing problems)
- [ ] Phase 2: Improvement (making things better)
- [ ] Phase 3: New Opportunity (completely new approach)
- [ ] Position your offer in Phase 3 (New Opportunity)

#### Distinguish Between Improvement and New Opportunity
- [ ] Understand Improvement fixes existing problems (bloody red ocean, price competition)
- [ ] Understand New Opportunity offers entirely different path/identity shift (blue ocean, desire-driven)
- [ ] Reframe your offer from "improvement" to "new opportunity"

#### Create an Opportunity Switch
- [ ] Identify their current opportunity and why it isn't working
- [ ] Introduce doubt about their current path
- [ ] Present the switch to a completely different approach
- [ ] Show benefits of the new opportunity

#### Focus on Desire, Not Pain
- [ ] Stop positioning around problems you solve
- [ ] Start positioning around who they'll become
- [ ] Use language of aspiration, transformation, and identity shift
- [ ] Paint a picture of new life, not just problem fixes

#### Position Your Offer in the Right Market
- [ ] Choose from:
  - [ ] Wealth Market (financial freedom, business success)
  - [ ] Health Market (fitness, beauty, longevity)
  - [ ] Relationship Market (love, friendship, belonging)

#### Create "Blue Ocean" Positioning
- [ ] Research what improvement offers exist
- [ ] Identify what new opportunity is NOT being offered
- [ ] Create positioning that stands out
- [ ] Make direct price comparison impossible

#### Emphasize Status Elevation
- [ ] Show how your opportunity elevates their status
- [ ] Demonstrate how they'll be seen differently by peers
- [ ] Create aspiration around who they'll become

#### Create Dream Replacement
- [ ] Help them see current dream may be limited
- [ ] Paint a NEW dream your opportunity enables
- [ ] Make your new dream more attractive
- [ ] Position your offer as the vehicle for this new dream

#### Stack vs. Switch Positioning
- [ ] Opportunity Switching: Take someone from current to new opportunity completely (most powerful)
- [ ] Opportunity Stacking: Add your offer to what they're already doing

#### Give Your Audience Permission to Dream
- [ ] Share stories of ordinary people who succeeded
- [ ] Show success doesn't require special talent
- [ ] Challenge their limiting beliefs
- [ ] Give them hope

#### Position Against the Enemy (System), Not Competitors
- [ ] Identify the core enemy (status quo, system, false belief)
- [ ] Show how that enemy keeps people stuck
- [ ] Position your opportunity as the revolution

#### Create Curiosity About Your New Opportunity
- [ ] Hint at what's possible without explaining everything
- [ ] Share stories that demonstrate power without full explanation
- [ ] Make details the payoff for buying

#### Use Five Curiosity Hooks
- [ ] Hidden benefits
- [ ] Counterintuitive strategies
- [ ] Personal stories
- [ ] New frameworks
- [ ] Simplified methods

### Secret #4: The Opportunity Switch

#### Step 1 - Work For Free to Generate Early Results
- [ ] Identify 10-20 people who represent your ideal customer
- [ ] Offer to work with them completely for free
- [ ] Help them get real results using your framework
- [ ] Document everything: process, results, transformation, feedback

#### Capture Results and Testimonials
- [ ] Collect before and after stories showing transformation
- [ ] Document quantifiable results (revenue, weight lost, etc.)
- [ ] Record video testimonials from satisfied participants
- [ ] Write case studies documenting the journey

#### Step 2 - Design The Vehicle (Six-Week Masterclass)
- [ ] Run a beta masterclass with 10-20 people:
  - [ ] Week 1: Foundation and framework introduction
  - [ ] Week 2: Deep dive into core concept #1
  - [ ] Week 3: Deep dive into core concept #2
  - [ ] Week 4: Deep dive into core concept #3
  - [ ] Week 5: Integration and practical application
  - [ ] Week 6: Results, Q&A, and final troubleshooting

#### Create Your Opportunity Switch Headline
- [ ] Use formula: "How to [RESULT THEY DESIRE MOST] Without [THING THEY FEAR MOST]"

#### Identify All Other Competing Opportunities
- [ ] Write down every vehicle your ideal customer has tried
- [ ] Document why each one hasn't worked
- [ ] Understand false beliefs associated with each

#### Position Your New Opportunity Against Competitors
- [ ] Acknowledge each competing opportunity
- [ ] Show its limitations
- [ ] Explain how yours overcomes those limitations

#### Use the Five Curiosity Hooks
- [ ] Hook #1: Little-Known, Big Differences
- [ ] Hook #2: The Sacred Secret
- [ ] Hook #3: Revisiting the Fundamentals
- [ ] Hook #4: The Crystal Ball Theory
- [ ] Hook #5: The Contrarian View

#### Step 3 - Give Them What They Want (The Ask Campaign)
- [ ] Stop trying to convince people what they NEED
- [ ] Start positioning what they WANT
- [ ] Focus on desire and aspiration, not logic

#### Create a Multi-Layered Offer Structure
- [ ] Layer 1: Free Content
- [ ] Layer 2: Lead Magnet
- [ ] Layer 3: Low-Ticket Offer ($7-$47)
- [ ] Layer 4: Mid-Ticket Offer ($500-$2,000)
- [ ] Layer 5: High-Ticket Offer ($5,000-$25,000+)

#### Make Your Opportunity Switch Irresistible
- [ ] Present core opportunity with standalone value
- [ ] Add bonuses one at a time
- [ ] Show combined total value being 10x the price
- [ ] Create urgency and scarcity

#### Identify Your Vehicle of Change
- [ ] Name your vehicle distinctly
- [ ] Create a clear framework
- [ ] Make it proprietary and unique

#### Show How Your New Opportunity Increases Status
- [ ] Show how it elevates their status
- [ ] Help them see themselves in a new identity
- [ ] Make clear how peers will perceive them differently

#### Create Opportunity Switch vs. Stack Strategy
- [ ] Use Switch for new customers, first offers
- [ ] Use Stack for existing customers wanting to expand

#### Get Them to Self-Identify
- [ ] Create a distinct identity for community members
- [ ] Give them a name to identify themselves
- [ ] Examples: "Funnel Hackers," "Life Coaches"

#### Create a "Title of Liberty"
- [ ] Write a manifesto explaining your movement
- [ ] Create a title sequence or video
- [ ] Use this rallying cry consistently

### Secret #5: The Big Domino

#### Understand the Big Domino Concept
- [ ] Identify the ONE core belief you must get someone to accept
- [ ] When believed, all other objections automatically become irrelevant

#### Create Your Big Domino Statement
- [ ] Use template: "If I can get people to believe that [YOUR NEW OPPORTUNITY] is the key to [RESULT THEY DESIRE MOST] and is only attainable through [YOUR SPECIFIC VEHICLE/FRAMEWORK], then all other objections and concerns become irrelevant and they have to give me money."

#### Understand What Gets Knocked Down
- [ ] When Big Domino falls, these objections become irrelevant:
  - [ ] Price objections
  - [ ] Feasibility objections
  - [ ] Timing objections
  - [ ] Comparison objections
  - [ ] Self-doubt objections

#### Make Your Big Domino Visual on Your Slide
- [ ] Create a slide stating: "My goal is to get you to believe that [YOUR BIG DOMINO STATEMENT]. I'm going to show you the frameworks that make it simple."

#### Use Your Origin Story to Knock Down the Big Domino
- [ ] Tell the story of how you personally discovered your Big Domino is true
- [ ] Show the struggle before you believed it
- [ ] Show the transformation after

#### Identify the Three Supporting Beliefs
- [ ] Belief #1 (Vehicle): "Is your opportunity the right one?"
- [ ] Belief #2 (Internal): "Can I personally succeed with this?"
- [ ] Belief #3 (External): "Are there external factors preventing success?"

#### Never Oversell Your Big Domino
- [ ] Use storytelling to plant the seed
- [ ] Let them come to the conclusion themselves
- [ ] Use Epiphany Bridge stories to gently guide them

#### Prepare Them Psychologically Before Presenting
- [ ] Intro slide should:
  - [ ] Justify their failures
  - [ ] Allay their fears
  - [ ] Throw rocks at their enemies
  - [ ] Confirm their suspicions
  - [ ] Encourage their dreams

#### Use Qualifying Questions Throughout
- [ ] "Does that make sense?"
- [ ] "Can you see why this works?"
- [ ] "Are you beginning to believe this could work for you?"

#### Create Multiple Epiphany Bridge Stories
- [ ] Secret #1 (Vehicle): Story about how you discovered vehicle works
- [ ] Secret #2 (Internal): Story about overcoming self-doubt
- [ ] Secret #3 (External): Story about overcoming external obstacles

#### Share Social Proof That Validates Your Big Domino
- [ ] Video testimonials from customers
- [ ] Specific metrics and results
- [ ] Stories of ordinary people who succeeded
- [ ] Before-and-after scenarios

#### Relate Your Big Domino to Their Desires
- [ ] Be specific about what they desire
- [ ] Show how your vehicle is the KEY
- [ ] Use emotional language
- [ ] Paint picture of life after believing

#### Filter Your Audience Using Your Big Domino
- [ ] Don't try to convince everyone
- [ ] Focus exclusively on those who resonate

#### Test and Refine Your Big Domino
- [ ] Record presentations and listen critically
- [ ] Test different framings
- [ ] Measure which version converts best

#### Never Deviate From Your Big Domino
- [ ] Don't add extra beliefs that distract
- [ ] Every story, slide, statistic should reinforce it
- [ ] Keep focus laser-sharp on one thing

### Secret #6: The Epiphany Bridge

#### Understand the Purpose of the Epiphany Bridge
- [ ] Guide them to their own conclusion through emotional storytelling
- [ ] Never convince directly; let them sell themselves

#### Learn the Five-Part Structure
- [ ] Part 1: The Introduction (Backstory)
- [ ] Part 2: The Journey and Conflict
- [ ] Part 3: The New Opportunity (The Epiphany)
- [ ] Part 4: The Framework
- [ ] Part 5: The Achievement and Transformation

#### Make Your Story About Your Audience
- [ ] Choose problems that mirror their challenges
- [ ] Use language that helps them recognize their struggles
- [ ] Focus on emotions they can relate to

#### Avoid Technical Jargon and "Technobabble"
- [ ] Use simple, conversational language
- [ ] Use "bridge" language: "It's kind of like..."
- [ ] Save technical details for after emotional buy-in

#### Focus on Emotions and Experiences
- [ ] Don't rush through emotional aspects
- [ ] Paint vivid pictures of pain points
- [ ] Describe relief and transformation you felt
- [ ] Use sensory details

#### Identify Your Core Epiphany
- [ ] Remember the specific moment that gave you your aha
- [ ] Identify who the guide was
- [ ] Recall what was happening and what changed
- [ ] Write it down in vivid detail

#### Use the Five-Part Structure Flexibly
- [ ] 60-second version: Touch on each part briefly
- [ ] 5-minute version: Go deeper with detail and emotion
- [ ] 60-minute version: Expand significantly with examples

#### Build a Story Inventory
- [ ] Create multiple versions:
  - [ ] Your Origin Story
  - [ ] Vehicle Framework Story
  - [ ] Internal Beliefs Story
  - [ ] External Beliefs Story

#### Connect Your Epiphany to Universal Human Truths
- [ ] Identify universal desire at heart of story (freedom, security, belonging)
- [ ] Show how epiphany addresses this universal need

#### Use Your Story to Create Pattern Interrupts
- [ ] Start with unexpected hook
- [ ] Build tension and curiosity
- [ ] Include twist that challenges assumptions

#### Practice Telling Your Story Repeatedly
- [ ] Tell to different audiences
- [ ] Record and review critically
- [ ] Notice which parts resonate
- [ ] Continuously refine

#### Make the Story About Transformation, Not Information
- [ ] Focus on emotional and philosophical shift
- [ ] Leave them wanting to learn the HOW
- [ ] Make it memorable and emotionally resonant

#### Use Story to Break False Beliefs
- [ ] Identify specific false belief to break
- [ ] Show how you held that same belief
- [ ] Show what changed your perspective

#### Maintain Honesty and Credibility
- [ ] Be honest about struggles and limitations
- [ ] Share realistic timelines
- [ ] Acknowledge what didn't work
- [ ] Use real numbers and specific examples

#### Create the Bridge From Emotion to Logic
- [ ] Use story for emotional buy-in first
- [ ] Then transition to logical proof (data, case studies)
- [ ] Emotion first, then logic

### Secret #7: The Hero's Two Journeys

#### Understand the Two Journeys Framework
- [ ] Journey of Achievement (External): Visible goal with finish line
- [ ] Journey of Transformation (Internal): Hidden change in who they are

#### Build Story Foundation with Character, Desire, and Conflict
- [ ] Character: Introduce hero clearly so audience cares
- [ ] Desire: Journey toward pleasure or away from pain (Win, Retrieve, Escape, Stop)
- [ ] Conflict: Introduce obstacles and villain

#### Phase 1 - Separation from Ordinary World
- [ ] Build rapport with the hero
- [ ] Introduce the desire
- [ ] Show the ordinary world before the journey

#### Identify the Core Desire
- [ ] Is it a win (achievement, status)?
- [ ] Retrieve (gain something)?
- [ ] Escape (freedom from something)?
- [ ] Stop (prevention of bad things)?

#### Phase 2 - The Journey, Conflict, and the Villain
- [ ] Introduce the villain (person, false belief, circumstances)
- [ ] Show resistance and failed attempts
- [ ] Increase the stakes
- [ ] Demonstrate power of the villain

#### Make the Villain Worthy
- [ ] Show villain's power and influence
- [ ] Demonstrate past victims
- [ ] Create genuine tension

#### Phase 3 - Meet the Mentor/Guide/Expert
- [ ] Position yourself as the guide
- [ ] Share your epiphany and frameworks
- [ ] Show credentials briefly
- [ ] Remain humble (audience is the hero)

#### Show the Hero Using the Framework
- [ ] Show obstacles overcome
- [ ] Share progressive results
- [ ] Build momentum
- [ ] Plant seeds of transformation

#### Phase 4 - The Achievement and Transformation
- [ ] Show external result (revenue, customers, weight lost)
- [ ] Show internal transformation (who they became, beliefs released)
- [ ] Demonstrate the rebirth
- [ ] Reveal the real victory

#### Make the Transformation Visible
- [ ] Describe how they were before (old beliefs, behaviors)
- [ ] Show the pivotal moments
- [ ] Reveal the new version
- [ ] Capture the emotion

#### Use Joseph Campbell's Hero's Journey
- [ ] 12-step simplified version:
  - [ ] 1. Ordinary world
  - [ ] 2. Call to adventure
  - [ ] 3. Refusal of the call
  - [ ] 4. Meeting the mentor
  - [ ] 5. Crossing first threshold
  - [ ] 6. Tests and allies
  - [ ] 7. Approach to innermost cave
  - [ ] 8. The ordeal
  - [ ] 9. The reward
  - [ ] 10. The road back
  - [ ] 11. The resurrection
  - [ ] 12. Return with the elixir

#### Apply Framework to Different Story Types
- [ ] Your origin story
- [ ] Customer transformation stories
- [ ] Case studies

#### Emphasize Transformation Over Outcome
- [ ] Don't just lead with "I made $1 million"
- [ ] Lead with confidence, identity shift
- [ ] Show how they'll feel and believe differently

#### Create Emotional Investment
- [ ] Use vivid sensory details
- [ ] Describe emotions, not just events
- [ ] Show struggle and vulnerability

#### Remember: Transformation Journey Is the Real Journey
- [ ] Even without external goal achieved, profound transformation creates powerful story
- [ ] When both complete, most compelling narratives

- [ ] Share the frustration

#### Stage 4 - Reveal Your Epiphany

- [ ] Name your framework

#### Stage 7 - Share Your Achievement

- [ ] Include multiple examples

#### Stage 8 - Describe Your Transformation
- [ ] Contrast "before" and "after" identity

#### Create Shortened Versions
- [ ] 60-second version
- [ ] 5-minute version
- [ ] 30-minute version

#### Use Script for Multiple Story Types
- [ ] Your origin story
- [ ] Vehicle framework story
- [ ] Internal beliefs story
- [ ] External beliefs story

#### Weave in Multiple Story Elements
- [ ] Use origin story upfront for Big Domino

#### Avoid Common Storytelling Mistakes
- [ ] Don't focus too much on yourself
- [ ] Don't use technical jargon

- [ ] Use analogies and metaphors

#### Test and Refine Your Script
- [ ] Record presentations
- [ ] Gather feedback
- [ ] Track conversion
- [ ] Make incremental improvements

### Secret #9: False Belief Patterns

#### Understand How False Beliefs Are Created
- [ ] Recognize beliefs come from experiences
- [ ] Understand brain creates narrative to make sense of experiences
- [ ] Acknowledge narrative becomes belief

#### Create Your False Belief Chain Inventory
- [ ] List 10+ false beliefs
- [ ] Categorize into Vehicle, Internal, External false beliefs

#### Trace the Origin Experience
- [ ] For each false belief, identify what experience caused it

#### Identify the False Story They're Telling Themselves
- [ ] Identify internal narratives like:
  - [ ] "I'm just not a disciplined person"
  - [ ] "That never works for people like me"
  - [ ] "The system is rigged against me"

#### Build Your Epiphany Bridge Story Inventory
- [ ] For each false belief, create counter-story showing:
  - [ ] You held same false belief
  - [ ] What changed your perspective
  - [ ] Your new belief after realization

#### Understand the Three Core False Beliefs
- [ ] Core #1 (Vehicle): "Is this the right vehicle?"
- [ ] Core #2 (Internal): "Can I succeed with this?"
- [ ] Core #3 (External): "Are there external forces holding me back?"

#### Understand Difference Between Beliefs and Facts
- [ ] Recognize beliefs feel true but may not be
- [ ] Understand logic won't change beliefs; stories will

#### Create Tangible Resources to Address Beliefs
- [ ] For Vehicle: Case studies, testimonials, metrics
- [ ] For Internal: Stories of ordinary people succeeding
- [ ] For External: Frameworks for overcoming obstacles

#### Use "You're Probably Thinking" Framework
- [ ] Structure: "You're probably thinking [FALSE BELIEF], right? Well, [TELL EPIPHANY BRIDGE STORY]."

#### Don't Try to Fix All False Beliefs
- [ ] Identify 3-5 MOST IMPORTANT false beliefs
- [ ] Focus on these core beliefs only

#### Use Customer Interviews
- [ ] Ask:
  - [ ] "What stopped you from trying this before?"
  - [ ] "What concerns do you have?"
  - [ ] "What's keeping you from taking action?"

#### Map False Beliefs to Specific Outcomes
- [ ] Show how beliefs prevented success
- [ ] Demonstrate belief becomes self-fulfilling prophecy

#### Create Different Messages for Different Segments
- [ ] Identify prevalent false belief in each segment
- [ ] Create customized messaging for each

#### Reinforce New Beliefs After Breaking Old Ones
- [ ] Don't just say old belief is wrong
- [ ] Introduce new belief to adopt
- [ ] Reinforce with multiple stories

#### Test Which False Beliefs Are Most Powerful
- [ ] Run different versions addressing different beliefs
- [ ] Track which converts best
- [ ] Focus on that belief going forward

- [ ] Use Stack Slide to show individual and combined value

#### Step 1: Identify Your Core Offer
- [ ] Pinpoint primary product or service

  - [ ] Exclusive access

#### Step 3: Determine Value of Each Item
- [ ] Assign realistic monetary value to each
- [ ] Show genuine standalone value

- [ ] Present each item one at a time

#### Step 6: Total Up Combined Value
- [ ] Tally combined value of entire stack
- [ ] Show total prominently

- [ ] Only after Stack Slide is complete
- [ ] Audience associates price with full offer

### Secret #12: The Perfect Webinar

#### Core Framework Structure (90 minutes)
- [ ] Minutes 0-15: Intro, Big Domino, Origin Story
- [ ] Minutes 15-30: Secret #1 (Vehicle Framework)
- [ ] Minutes 30-45: Secret #2 (Internal Beliefs)
- [ ] Minutes 45-60: Secret #3 (External Beliefs)
- [ ] Minutes 60-90: Stack and Close

#### Start with Engaging Introduction
- [ ] Open with music, videos, effects
- [ ] Build rapport with engaging questions in chat
- [ ] Ask "Where are you from?"

#### Identify and Communicate Your Big Domino
- [ ] First 15 minutes for capturing attention and trust
- [ ] Deliver compelling origin story
- [ ] Knock over the Big Domino
- [ ] Explain why you're uniquely qualified

#### Present Three Secrets
