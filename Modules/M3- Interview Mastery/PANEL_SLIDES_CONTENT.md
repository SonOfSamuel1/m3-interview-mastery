## SLIDE 18: Panel Interview Overview & Strategic Context

**Title:** Final Panel Presentation

**Subtitle:** Understanding Panel Interviews

**Content:**

**Overview**
The panel interview represents the final evaluation stage where you'll present to multiple decision-makers simultaneously. This interview assesses your strategic thinking, executive presence, leadership potential, and ability to navigate complex stakeholder dynamics. Panel interviews for $200k+ roles often include a formal business presentation component (30-60-90 day plan or strategic proposal).

**What Panel Interviews Evaluate**
1. Strategic thinking and business acumen
2. Executive presence and confidence under pressure
3. Ability to engage diverse stakeholders with competing priorities
4. Communication clarity and persuasion skills
5. Cultural fit at leadership/senior level
6. Preparedness and professionalism

**Key Reminders**
1. Panel members have different priorities—address each stakeholder's concerns
2. Your presentation quality reflects your work quality
3. This is your opportunity to demonstrate leadership-level capabilities
4. Balance confidence with humility and collaboration
5. Prepare for both presentation delivery and rigorous Q&A

## SLIDE 19: Preparation - Before the Panel Interview

**Title:** Final Panel Presentation

**Subtitle:** Actions to Take Before

**Content:**

**Research & Intelligence Gathering**
1. LinkedIn research on every panel member (role, background, priorities, recent posts)
2. Identify each stakeholder's likely concerns and evaluation criteria
3. Research company's current challenges, initiatives, and strategic direction
4. Review recent news, earnings reports, leadership announcements
5. Understand reporting structure and how the role interfaces with panel members

**Presentation Development**
6. Create 30-60-90 day plan or strategic business case presentation (10-12 slides)
7. Prepare customized talking points addressing each panel member's domain
8. Develop data-driven recommendations with clear ROI/impact metrics
9. Anticipate objections and prepare thoughtful responses
10. Design professional slides (simple, visual, executive-appropriate)

**Practice & Logistics**
11. Rehearse presentation 5+ times (aim for 15-20 minute delivery)
12. Practice with a timer—stay within time limits
13. Prepare technical setup (if virtual: test screen share, lighting, audio)
14. Plan arrival time (15 minutes early for in-person, 5 minutes early for virtual)
15. Bring physical copies of presentation handouts (if in-person)
16. Prepare strategic questions to ask each panel member

## SLIDE 20: Execution - During the Panel Interview

**Title:** Final Panel Presentation

**Subtitle:** Actions to Take During

**Content:**

**Opening & Presence**
1. Greet each panel member individually with confident handshake/acknowledgment
2. Open presentation with compelling hook that frames the opportunity
3. Establish credibility early—briefly reinforce your relevant expertise
4. Project executive presence: confident posture, vocal clarity, steady pacing
5. Maintain energy and enthusiasm throughout (avoid monotone delivery)

**Stakeholder Engagement**
6. Distribute eye contact evenly across all panel members
7. Read body language and engagement levels—adjust if needed
8. Address questions to the person asking but include others in your response
9. Acknowledge different perspectives when panel members have competing priorities
10. Use panel members' names when referencing their specific domains

**Presentation Excellence**
11. Stay within time limits (respect their calendars)
12. Use data and specific examples to support recommendations
13. Handle interruptions gracefully—welcome questions as engagement
14. If you don't know an answer, commit to follow-up rather than guessing
15. Close with clear next steps and express genuine enthusiasm for the opportunity

## SLIDE 21: Follow-Up - After the Panel Interview

**Title:** Final Panel Presentation

**Subtitle:** Actions to Take After

**Content:**

**Immediate Actions (Within 24 Hours)**
1. Send individual thank-you emails to each panel member (not group email)
2. Reference specific conversation points from the presentation (show you listened)
3. Address any questions you committed to researching during the interview
4. Attach any supporting materials promised during discussion
5. Reiterate your enthusiasm and specific interest in the role/company

**Strategic Follow-Up**
6. Connect with panel members on LinkedIn with personalized connection notes
7. If panel suggested next steps, confirm timeline and your availability
8. Send brief thank-you to recruiter/HR contact summarizing your interest
9. Document key insights from interview (valuable for negotiation later)
10. Note any concerns raised during Q&A—prepare stronger responses for final conversations

**Maintaining Momentum**
11. Follow up on timeline if you haven't heard back within specified timeframe
12. Continue nurturing the relationship with occasional value-add touchpoints
13. Stay visible and engaged (thoughtful LinkedIn engagement with company content)
14. Be patient but persistent—senior hiring decisions take time
15. Prepare for final conversations or reference checks that may follow

## SLIDE 22: Panel Interview Questions to Be Prepared For

**Title:** Final Panel Presentation

**Subtitle:** Questions to Be Prepared For:

**Content:**

**Strategic Thinking & Vision**
1. Walk us through your 30-60-90 day plan. What are your key priorities and why?
2. What's your assessment of our biggest challenge right now, and how would you approach it?
3. How would you measure success in this role during your first year?
4. What strategic initiatives would you prioritize, and what would you intentionally not focus on initially?
5. How do you balance short-term wins with long-term strategic goals?

**Leadership & Influence**
6. How do you build credibility and influence when joining a new organization?
7. Describe a time when you had to lead through significant change. What was your approach?
8. How do you handle situations where senior stakeholders have competing priorities?
9. Tell us about a time you had to influence upward (convince leadership to change direction).

**Team & Collaboration**
10. How would you approach building relationships with this team and key stakeholders?
11. What's your management philosophy, and how do you develop high-performing teams?
12. How do you handle underperformance while maintaining team morale?

**Domain Expertise**
13. Based on what you know about our business, what opportunities are we missing?
14. How would you apply your experience from [previous company/industry] to our context?
15. What makes you uniquely qualified for this role compared to other candidates?

## SLIDE 23: Panel Interview Answer Examples

**Title:** Final Panel Presentation

**Subtitle:** Answer Examples

**Content:**

**Where to Find Example Answers**
For detailed example answers to panel interview questions, including 30-60-90 day plan templates and presentation frameworks, refer to:

**Active Offer Scripts Workbook - Pages 18-25**
• Strategic question response frameworks
• 30-60-90 day plan structure and examples
• Stakeholder engagement strategies
• Executive presence techniques
• Panel Q&A best practices

**Key Answer Frameworks to Use**

**For Strategic Questions:**
• State the challenge/opportunity clearly
• Present your analytical process (how you'd assess)
• Offer 2-3 specific recommendations with rationale
• Quantify expected impact where possible
• Acknowledge risks and mitigation strategies

**For Leadership Questions:**
• Use STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
• Emphasize collaboration and stakeholder engagement
• Highlight both the outcome and what you learned
• Connect past experience to future application in this role

**For Presentation Defense:**
• Listen fully to the question/concern
• Acknowledge valid points before responding
• Use data to support your position
• Show flexibility—"Here's why I proposed X, but I'm open to Y if..."
• Demonstrate strategic thinking in real-time
