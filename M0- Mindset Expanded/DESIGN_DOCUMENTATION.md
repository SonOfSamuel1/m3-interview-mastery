## Brand Style Guide

### Visual Design

**Color Palette:**
- Background: #F5F5F5 (Light gray)
- Text: #000000 (Pure black)
- No accent colors or gradients

**Typography:**
- Font Family: Helvetica (system font)
- Title Slides: 120pt, centered
- Content Slide Titles: 80pt, left-aligned
- Body Text: 52pt, left-aligned with bullet points
- Small Text: 44pt (used sparingly for dense content)

**Layout Principles:**
- Minimalist, clean aesthetic
- 30-40% white space on each slide
- Left-aligned content with consistent margins (100px left/right, 80px top)
- Bullet points indented 60px from left margin
- Line spacing: 1.4x for optimal readability
- No decorative elements, icons, or graphics

**Slide Types:**
1. **Title Slides:** Large centered text, used for lesson openers
2. **Content Slides:** Title at top, 4-6 bullet points maximum

### Design Rationale

The minimalist design serves several strategic purposes:

1. **Professional Authority:** Clean, uncluttered slides signal sophistication and confidence
2. **Focus on Content:** No visual distractions from the core learning material
3. **Presentation Flexibility:** Instructor becomes the visual focal point in video lessons
4. **Accessibility:** High contrast (black on light gray) ensures readability
5. **Premium Positioning:** Mirrors the aesthetic of high-end consulting firms and executive education programs

## Content Philosophy

### High-Ticket Course Design Principles

1. **Depth Over Breadth:** Each concept is explored thoroughly with practical application
2. **Evidence-Based:** Grounded in psychology, behavioral science, and career development research
3. **Actionable:** Every lesson includes specific exercises and implementation steps
4. **Premium Value:** Content addresses mindset gaps that cost professionals $50k-$200k+ in lost compensation
5. **Transformation-Focused:** Designed to shift identity, not just provide information

### Pedagogical Approach

**Scaffolded Learning:**
- Lesson 1: Foundation (understanding the problem)
- Lesson 2: Self-Awareness (identifying personal barriers)
- Lesson 3: Skill-Building (developing confidence)
- Lesson 4: Framework Application (7 Success Habits)
- Lesson 5: System Design (implementation roadmap)

**Engagement Techniques:**
- Provocative questions that challenge assumptions
- Real-world examples relevant to sales professionals
- Specific, quantifiable outcomes ($200k-$500k+ roles)
- Reframing exercises with immediate applicability
- Clear action steps for each lesson

## Implementation Recommendations

### For Video Production

1. **Timing:** Each slide should be displayed for 30-60 seconds on average
2. **Narration Style:** Conversational, authoritative, with personal anecdotes
3. **Visual Aids:** Instructor on camera with slides in background (70/30 split)
4. **Pacing:** Pause after key concepts to let them land
5. **Engagement:** Ask rhetorical questions, reference student situations
